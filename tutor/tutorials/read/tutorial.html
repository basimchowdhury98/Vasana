Module 05

Adjust by Material Type

Learn an adaptive reading system instead of one fixed speed. For fantasy and other immersive fiction, read mostly at a steady flow pace and slow down only for unfamiliar worldbuilding, emotionally important scenes, or places where your understanding slips. For technical software books, preview the chapter, code samples, headings, and examples first, then move between scanning, skimming, and full reading based on whether you need orientation, a specific answer, or deep understanding. For dense explanatory text, start with a clear purpose, map the structure, and deliberately slow for definitions, claims, and high-information passages while moving faster through transitions, repetition, and familiar material. The module also reinforces that good readers adjust pace continuously, use previewing to reduce zoning out and unnecessary rereading, and do not try to force away subvocalization at the expense of comprehension.
