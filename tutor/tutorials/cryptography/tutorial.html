Applied Cryptography for Experienced Developers

A practical cryptography tutorial for experienced developers focused on choosing the right primitive, implementing safe workflows, and diagnosing trust failures in real systems. It covers hashes and HMACs, authenticated encryption, hybrid public-key designs, digital signatures, TLS validation, internal PKI issuance, SSH trust, and OpenPGP operations with an emphasis on hands-on practice and misuse analysis.

Module 01

Hashes, Integrity, and MAC Boundaries

Build a small integrity-checking workflow for files and messages, then compare plain hashes, keyed hashes, and HMAC in practice. The hands-on outcome is a lab that demonstrates why raw hashes do not provide authenticity, where MAC misuse happens, and how to choose between checksum, hash, HMAC, and password-hashing or KDF-style tools for different threat models.

Module 02

Authenticated Symmetric Encryption
